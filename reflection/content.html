Reflection on FSW-105 at Bryan University.

Challenges I had to overcome:

At the start of FSW-105 I was not familiar with JavaScript at all. It looked similar to GML, which I was familiar with, but I had never tried JavaScript.
I was able to overcome my lack of knowledge in JavaScript with the help of my instructor Mr. Rajani. It was also super helpful to have my awesome classmates,
Davey, Churchill, Petrina, Michael and Bobby to help me out. We supported each other and helped ensure each of us made it through this course with as much
understanding as possible. I hope we continue to do so throughout our college experience.

How I handled the workload!:

Prior to the start of college I was working a very demanding job. I decided to change jobs for the duration that I was in college. Unfortunately the job I
swapped to was just as demanding and college had already begun. I ended up joining a local delivery service where I could work on my own time in order to better
plan for my college assignments and manage my time. While it has been a struggle financially we have managed to keep our heads afloat and I have been able to
dedicate more than enough time to college to stay ahead in all of my coursework.

My favorite things about the course:

Its hard to pick my single favorite thing about what I learned in FSW-105. My favorite methods were .slice(), .split() and .splice() because of how similar they
are but how differently they operate. My favorite topic was probably the constructor function / prototype paradigm because of how useful it seems to be in real
world application. Lastly my favorite assignment was the text-based RPG assignment in node because I'm a huge nerd for fantasy games.
